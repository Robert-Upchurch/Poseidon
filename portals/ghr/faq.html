GHR FAQ

Common questions about the J-1 visa, sponsors, housing, pay, and healthcare.

What is the J-1 visa?

The J-1 is the US non-immigrant visa category for Cultural Exchange visitors. It is governed by 22 CFR Part 62 and administered by the US Department of State. Hospitality candidates typically apply under the Trainee, Intern, or Summer Work Travel programs.

What is a sponsor?

An accredited sponsor is a US Department of State-designated organization that issues your DS-2019 form, monitors your program, and supports you during your stay. GHR partners with three accredited sponsors: Alliance Abroad, CIEE, and Greenheart.

Is housing included?

For most placements, GHR helps you confirm housing before you travel. Cost varies by location and is typically paid by the participant out of monthly earnings. Your tracking link shows your housing assignment summary once confirmed. Exact addresses are released only after your placement deposit is received.

How does payroll work?

You are paid by your US host employer, on the same payroll cycle as their other staff. For tipped roles, US tipping norms apply. Land hospitality roles in Southeast Asia follow local payroll conventions and are typically paid monthly.

What about taxes?

J-1 participants are generally subject to US federal and state income tax on US-earned wages. Social Security and Medicare taxes (FICA) are typically NOT withheld for J-1 visa holders during their first two years. We strongly recommend consulting a licensed tax professional. GHR does not provide tax advice.

Is healthcare included?

Yes. US Department of State regulations require every J-1 participant to carry sponsor-approved medical insurance for the duration of the program. Your sponsor enrolls you and the cost is deducted from your first paychecks or paid upfront, depending on the sponsor.

How long is a typical program?

Cultural Exchange programs run 4 to 12 months depending on the visa category and host employer. Land hospitality contracts in Southeast Asia run 6 to 12 months and are renewable.

Can I extend my J-1?

Extensions are governed by sponsor policy and the original DS-2019 maximum duration. Some categories allow extension up to the program category cap; others do not. Discuss extension with your sponsor and host employer before applying.

What is the 2-year home residency rule?

Some J-1 participants are subject to a 2-year home residency requirement after their program ends, meaning they must spend two years in their home country before applying for certain other US visas. Whether this applies depends on the program category and your country's Skills List status. We will tell you whether you are subject during intake.

Can I travel inside the US during the program?

Yes, during your authorized off-time. Domestic travel does not require a new visa. International travel may require visa revalidation depending on your circumstances - check with your sponsor before booking.
