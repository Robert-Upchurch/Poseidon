Your path

From application to placement.

Seven steps for J-1 Cultural Exchange. Land hospitality skips the visa steps.

1

Apply

Complete our 10-minute online intake. Tell us about your education, English level, country preferences, and which track interests you - Cultural Exchange in the US, or land hospitality in Southeast Asia. You receive a tracking link by email.

2

Sponsor matched

For Cultural Exchange: we match you to an accredited sponsor (Alliance Abroad, CIEE, or Greenheart) based on your profile and the host employer. For land hospitality: we connect you directly with our regional partner.

3

DS-2019 issued

Cultural Exchange only. Your sponsor issues the DS-2019 form, which authorizes you to apply for a J-1 visa at the US consulate. Land hospitality candidates can skip this step.

4

Interview

Video interview with the host employer. For Cultural Exchange, this is often paired with a sponsor pre-screening. Most candidates clear interview within two weeks.

5

Visa appointment

Cultural Exchange only. You book your J-1 visa appointment at the US consulate. We provide a complete prep packet and walk you through the consular interview questions before you arrive.

6

Travel & arrival

Travel booked, housing confirmed. You receive an arrival packet with your first-week schedule. For Cultural Exchange candidates we also schedule a sponsor check-in at week one.

7

Placement

Begin your role. Cultural Exchange programs typically run 4 to 12 months. Land hospitality contracts run 6 to 12 months. Through your tracking link, you can stay in touch with GHR for the duration of your placement.
