Cruise career FAQ

Common questions about life at sea, contracts, documents, and pay.

What visa do I need to work on a cruise ship?

For most cruise contracts on US-itinerary ships you will need a US C1/D visa. For other regions different visa rules apply. CTI helps you identify and obtain the right visa for your assigned route.

How long is a typical contract?

Contracts most often run six to nine months continuously at sea, followed by a paid leave period of one to three months. Length varies by cruise line and department.

How is pay structured?

Hospitality and food & beverage roles often combine a base salary with gratuities or commission. Deck and technical roles are typically salaried. Pay is generally USD-denominated and paid monthly. Accommodation, meals, and uniforms aboard are provided by the cruise line.

What training will I receive?

Cruise lines run mandatory induction on join. Before that you need STCW Basic Safety (4 modules, recognized worldwide). For some roles you will need additional certifications - we will tell you exactly what is required for your role and route, and we will point you to recognized training providers if you do not have them yet.

Do I get time off during the contract?

Most roles include rest periods between shifts and time off in port. Shore leave depends on the role and the day-of-port operations. Between contracts you typically receive 1 to 3 months of paid leave.

What is the seaman's book and do I need one?

The seaman's book (sometimes called a Continuous Discharge Certificate) is an internationally recognized record of your sea service. Most cruise lines require one to embark. CTI guides you through the issuing authority in your country.

What about taxes?

Tax treatment for seafarers varies significantly by country of citizenship and country of residence. CTI does not provide tax advice. We strongly recommend you consult a licensed tax professional in your home country before starting your contract.

Can I bring family with me?

In general, no. Crew accommodations and visa rules do not permit family travel during a contract. Some senior officers on certain lines may have limited family-visit privileges. Confirm with your line if this matters to you.

What happens if I am injured or fall ill at sea?

Cruise lines maintain onboard medical facilities and shore-side medical agreements at most ports. Crew members are covered by the cruise line's contracted medical plan for the duration of the contract. Pre-existing conditions need to be disclosed at the pre-employment medical exam (ENG-1 or PEME).

How long does the whole process take?

From application to embarkation typically takes 6 to 12 weeks, depending on document readiness and the line's hiring calendar. Candidates with full documents already in hand often clear in 4 to 6 weeks.
