# CTI Group — Brand Voice Guide

**Version 1.0** · Issued May 19, 2026 · Owner: Robert Upchurch, President & CEO
**Status: Canon — all CTI marketing references this document.**

## How to Use This Guide

This is the parent document for all CTI Group marketing. Every post, video script, email, brochure, landing page, and partner communication — across all five divisions — inherits its voice, tone, and rules from here. Content is checked against this guide before it goes out.

The purpose is consistency. CTI has operated for over forty years and competes as an institutional partner, not a vendor. This guide makes the voice repeatable so that whether a message comes from corporate, J-1, cruise staffing, uniforms, promotions, or marine travel, it unmistakably sounds like CTI.

## 1. Who CTI Is

CTI Group Worldwide Services Inc. is a U.S.-based international exchange and staffing organization headquartered in Weirsdale, Florida, with 40+ years of international business experience and a global footprint including offices in Bali and Thailand and business associations in Myanmar and Malaysia.

Five divisions:
- **J-1 Visa** — international exchange and placement programs
- **Cruise Ship Staffing (C1/D)** — maritime crew sourcing and placement
- **UNO Uniforms** — uniform supply
- **Baron Promotions** — promotional products and branded merchandise
- **CTI Marine Travel** — travel services for the maritime sector

**Brand position: institutional peer, not vendor.** Every communication reinforces this.

## 3. Voice Principles

1. **Lead with proof, not adjectives.** Replace unsupported adjectives with facts.
2. **Speak as a peer to decision-makers.** One professional to another.
3. **Be concise and direct.** Cut any sentence that can be cut.
4. **Stay calm and never pressure.** No urgency tactics, no hard sells.
5. **Respect the division firewall.** Cruise/maritime and J-1/exchange are never blended in one piece of content. Hard rule — see Section 7.

## 4. Tone by Audience

| Audience | Tone |
|---|---|

| Candidates & applicants | Clear, encouraging, practical. Warmer, never salesy. |
| Employers & cruise lines | Operational, precise, numbers forward. |
| General public / social | Accessible and confident. Plain-language proof. |
| Internal / staff | Direct and instructional. Clarity over polish. |

## 5. Words and Phrases

**Uses:** experienced, established, international, placement, partner, infrastructure, sponsor, program, compliance, sourcing, decades, operational.

**Avoids:**
- Hype: game-changing, revolutionary, world-class, best-in-class, cutting-edge
- Pressure: act now, limited time, don't miss out, hurry
- Vague filler: passionate, synergy, leverage (verb), empower (no object)
- Hedging: we think we might, probably, hopefully

**Naming:** Full legal name on first/formal reference (CTI Group Worldwide Services Inc.); "CTI" or "CTI Group" thereafter. Divisions named exactly. "J-1" always hyphenated; "C1/D" exactly so.

## 7. The Division Firewall (Hard Rule)

CTI's cruise/maritime work (C1/D, MLC-governed) and J-1/exchange work (U.S. exchange-visitor rules) operate in separate regulatory and audience worlds. They are never combined in a single piece of marketing content.

1. No post, video, email, brochure, or page promotes cruise staffing and J-1 placement together.
2. Candidate-facing funnels are entirely separate — pages, forms, CTAs.
3. Corporate content may note both divisions exist in CTI's portfolio, but must not cross-sell or blend messaging.
4. Any draft that blurs this line is rejected and rewritten before publication.

Non-negotiable; overrides any creative or campaign consideration.

## 9. The One-Line Test

Before publishing, all six must be yes:
1. Leads with proof, not adjectives?
2. Sounds like an experienced peer, not a vendor pitching?
3. Every sentence necessary?
4. Free of hype and pressure language?
5. Respects the cruise / J-1 firewall completely?
6. Would a forty-year institution sign its name to it?
